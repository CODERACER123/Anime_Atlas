Kaiju No. 8

Kafka Hibino has failed the Defence Force entrance exam twice and now spends his days cleaning up kaiju corpses. After being infected by a small kaiju, he gains the ability to transform into an impossibly powerful monster himself — Kaiju No. 8 — while remaining human inside. Now he must hide his power from the very force he's trying to join.

Story overview

Japan's most wanted kaiju is trying to join the Defence Force.

Act 1: Kafka's Transformation

Kafka passes the Defence Force exam — just after gaining the ability to become a kaiju that the force is actively hunting. He joins the Third Division under the legendary Mina Ashiro — the woman he promised to fight alongside as a child.

Act 2: Kaiju No. 9

An intelligent kaiju begins appearing — capable of strategy, stealing Daiskaiju parts to augment itself, and targeting specific Defence Force members. It has an interest in Kafka's transformation that goes beyond coincidence.

Act 3: The Secret Exposed

Kafka's transformation is eventually witnessed by colleagues. The question shifts from "will he be discovered" to "what does it mean that a human can become a kaiju without losing himself — and what does the Defence Force do with that?"

Ongoing: War Escalation

The kaiju threat escalates dramatically as humanoid kaiju appear in increasing numbers. Kafka and his division are at the centre of a conflict that may require redefining what humanity's relationship with kaiju actually means.

Fun facts

Behind Japan's most popular Jump+ manga.

The manga broke Shonen Jump+ records.

Naoya Matsumoto's Kaiju No. 8 became Jump+ 's fastest manga to reach 10 million views — before the anime was announced, purely through manga readership.

Kafka is 32 — a rare adult protagonist in shonen action.

His age is deliberately central to the story. The series explicitly engages with what it means to pursue a dream when you're well past the "age when you should have given up."

Production I.G animated the TV series (2024).

Known for Ghost in the Shell, Haikyuu!!, and Attack on Titan, Production I.G's involvement raised the anime's production profile significantly before a single episode aired.
