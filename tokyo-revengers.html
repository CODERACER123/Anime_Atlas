Tokyo Revengers

24-year-old loser Takemichi Hanagaki learns his middle school girlfriend Hinata was killed by a criminal gang. Then he time-leaps back twelve years. His mission: infiltrate the Tokyo Manji Gang and change the future — one impossibly difficult, perfectly timed punch at a time.

Suggested rating: TV-14 Suggested age: 14+ Content note: gang violence, death

Core Themes

  • Loyalty — which bonds survive time and betrayal
  • The past vs. the present self you've become
  • Whether who you were at 14 still defines you at 24

Story overview

A crybaby time-traveller trying to save everyone.

Act 1: The First Leap

Takemichi enters middle school 2005 in his own body — and discovers he has to work through the social hierarchy of the Tokyo Manji Gang to get close to key figures and alter the chain of events leading to Hinata's death.

Act 2: Mikey and Draken

The gang's two leaders define its soul: Mikey (Manjiro Sano) is charismatic, terrifyingly strong, and concealing a deep wound; Draken is loyal, sensible, and the one Takemichi desperately tries to keep alive through the first arc.

Act 3: Valhalla and Tenjiku

Rival gangs with their own compelling leaders challenge Toman repeatedly. Each arc introduces new antagonists whose motivations are given enough space to be genuinely complex — not mere obstacles but characters with their own histories.

Finale: The Final Loop

The series' final arcs (Black Dragon, Brahman, Kanto Manji) escalate toward a definitive timeline — and a conclusion that attempts to resolve every repeated sacrifice Takemichi has made across his time-leaping journey.

Fun facts

Behind the bike gangs and the butterfly effect.

Takemichi is intentionally written as the weakest protagonist in the room.

He loses most fights. His power isn't strength — it's stubbornness and emotional intelligence. The series is specifically about what those qualities achieve that physical dominance cannot.

The series captures Japan's 2000s bosozoku delinquent subculture.

The gang aesthetics, hairstyles, and territorial rivalry rituals are drawn from the actual bosozoku motorcycle gang culture of that era — giving the middle-school drama a specific nostalgic texture.

Mikey became one of the most beloved characters in modern manga.

His combination of overwhelming combat ability, genuine weakness hidden underneath, and tragic arc made Mikey a fan-favourite who transcended the series itself.

Tokyo Manji Gang leadership

  • Draken (Ken Ryuguji) — co-founder, the conscience
  • Chifuyu Matsuno — Takemichi's only confidant
  • Takemichi Hanagaki — the time-leaping crybaby hero
