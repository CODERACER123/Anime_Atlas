Classroom of the Elite

At the prestigious Tokyo Metropolitan Advanced Nurturing School, students are ranked by merit and compete for points. Behind the blank facade of Kiyotaka Ayanokoji hides a calculating genius who refuses to reveal his true nature.

Story overview

A school where nothing is as it seems.

Act 1: Class D's Disadvantage

Ayanokoji is placed in Class D — the lowest ranked class, despised by the rest of the school. Students receive monthly points as currency. Class D has been burning through theirs recklessly, unaware of how the system really works.

Act 2: The System Revealed

The school's true structure becomes clear: classes compete against each other in special exams. The points, privileges, and class rankings are all part of a social experiment designed to forge Japan's elite.

Act 3: Ayanokoji Moves the Pieces

While appearing to be an ordinary, unambitious student, Ayanokoji orchestrates events behind the scenes with a level of foresight that unsettles anyone who catches a glimpse of it.

Season 2 & 3: Who Is Ayanokoji?

As rivals grow stronger and the backstory of the "White Room" — a place that shaped Ayanokoji into what he is — is slowly revealed, the stakes of the game become deeply personal.

Fun facts

Behind the elite school's iron curtain.

Based on a popular light novel series.

The anime only covers a portion of the ongoing light novels, which delve much deeper into Ayanokoji's chilling backstory and methodology.

Ayanokoji is one of anime's most discussed protagonists.

His true motives and the extent of his abilities are subjects of intense fan theory — he consistently tops "most calculated anime character" lists.

Adapted multiple times.

After the 2017 anime, a second and third season were produced following renewed fan demand generated by the light novel's popularity.
