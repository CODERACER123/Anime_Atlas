Slam Dunk

Delinquent Hanamichi Sakuragi joins his school's basketball team purely to impress a girl — and discovers that his freakish athleticism, once trained, might make him something genuinely extraordinary. The series that defined an era of sports manga.

Story overview

The natural with the worst attitude and the biggest heart.

Act 1: Sakuragi Joins Shohoku

After 50 rejections from girls citing his delinquent reputation, Sakuragi meets Haruko Akagi — who loves basketball. He immediately claims to be a basketball genius and joins the team. He is not a genius. Yet.

Act 2: Learning the Basics

Under reluctant coaching — mainly from Haruko's captain brother Takenori — Sakuragi learns dribbling, passing, and shooting. His physical gifts are undeniable; his knowledge gap is enormous. Both are played for maximum effect.

Act 3: Interhigh Tournament

Shohoku — widely considered a no-hope school — begins upsetting ranked opponents. Ace Kaede Rukawa is simultaneously the team's best player and its most frustrating personality. The team chemistry is fractious and brilliant.

Nationals: Vs. Sannoh

The match against national powerhouse Sannoh in the manga's climax is considered one of the greatest sports sequences ever written — Sakuragi's final act in the game is among manga's most iconic moments.

Fun facts

The manga that put basketball on Japan's map.

The 2022 film "The First Slam Dunk" was a global phenomenon.

Takehiko Inoue's animated film adaptation — released nearly 25 years after the manga ended — became one of the highest-grossing anime films ever made.

Basketball participation in Japan spiked after Slam Dunk's serialisation.

The Japan Basketball Association credited the manga with dramatically increasing youth interest in the sport through the 1990s.

Takehiko Inoue went on to create Vagabond and Real.

The dramatic artistic growth from Slam Dunk's energetic comic style to Vagabond's painterly realism shows one of manga's most remarkable artistic evolutions.
