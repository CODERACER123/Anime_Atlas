Kuroko's Basketball

The legendary "Generation of Miracles" — five once-in-a-decade prodigies who once played together — have scattered to rival schools. The phantom sixth member, the invisible Kuroko Tetsuya, teams with powerhouse freshman Kagami to build a team that can beat them all.

Story overview

Beat the prodigies. One by one.

Season 1: Finding Their Game

Seirin High forms around Kagami and Kuroko. Their unique combination — Kagami's explosive physical ability and Kuroko's misdirection passing — begins to upset established powerhouses. Their first loss to the Generation is instructive.

Season 2: The Winter Cup Begins

The Inter-High tournament brings Seirin against Midorima, Kise, and others. Each GoM member has a superpower that borders on superhuman — the series escalates into highly stylised basketball battles.

Season 3: Akashi and the Emperor Eye

Generation captain Akashi Seijuro is the final boss — his Emperor Eye allows him to see the future of each play. Defeating him requires Kuroko and Kagami to reach a state beyond their individual limits.

Extra Game: The Jabberwock

An American street basketball team publicly humiliates Japan's players — prompting an unofficial team of the Generation of Miracles to form and fight back in a rematch that is pure catharsis.

Fun facts

Behind the phantom player and his team.

Each Generation of Miracles has a colour theme.

Kuroko (teal), Kise (yellow), Midorima (green), Aomine (navy), Murasakibara (purple), Akashi (red) — the colour-coding is deliberate and runs through the entire series.

Kuroko's ability is intentionally broken in theory.

Vanishing Drive, Phantom Shot, and Misdirection work because defenders forget Kuroko exists — a power built entirely on psychological invisibility, not physicality.

The series has a massive female fanbase.

Kuroko no Basket became one of the defining sports titles of the BL-adjacent "fujoshi" fanbase in the early 2010s, driving enormous merchandise and doujinshi sales.
