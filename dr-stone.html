Dr. Stone

After a mysterious flash petrifies all of humanity, genius teen Senku Ishigami revives 3,700 years later and sets out to use the power of science to rebuild civilization from zero.

Story overview

Ten billion percent: rebuilding humanity with science.

Act 1: Stone World Awakening

Senku and athletic best friend Taiju revive in a world of stone statues and untouched nature. Using nitric acid, they unpetrify themselves and begin sourcing raw materials to rebuild society.

Act 2: Kingdom of Science vs. Kingdom of Might

Tsukasa — the world's strongest high schooler — opposes Senku's vision, wanting to build a world of pure might. Their conflict drives the first major arc.

Act 3: Mantle of Science Spreads

Senku builds a village, invents antibiotics, creates phones, and even attempts a space program — each invention is explained with real science, making viewers genuinely learn as they watch.

Finale: Why Was Humanity Petrified?

The series evolves into a globe-spanning mystery as Senku's Kingdom of Science hunts for the source of the petrification — and faces an enemy far greater than Tsukasa.

Fun facts

The science behind Dr. Stone is surprisingly real.

The science is actually accurate.

Riichiro Inagaki consulted with chemists and scientists to ensure Senku's inventions — from sulfa drugs to smartphones — use real, reproducible chemistry.

Viewers have replicated inventions from the show.

Fans have successfully followed the show's instructions to make gunpowder, alcohol, and basic electronics. It's essentially a science textbook in anime form.

"Ten billion percent!" is Senku's catchphrase.

This exclamation became one of anime's most recognizable character signatures, accompanying every scientific breakthrough.

Notable inventions

  • Nitric acid de-petrification fluid
  • Sulfa drugs (first antibiotics)
  • Vacuum tube telephone network
  • Rocketry and space program
