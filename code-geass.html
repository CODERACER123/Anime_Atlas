Code Geass

Exiled Britannian prince Lelouch vi Britannia gains the power of absolute obedience — the Geass — and uses it to secretly lead a rebellion against the empire that destroyed his mother.

Story overview

A prince behind a mask, reshaping the world.

Act 1: The Power of Kings

Lelouch, living as a student in Area 11 (Japan), encounters a mysterious girl named C.C. who grants him the Geass — the ability to give any person one absolute command.

Act 2: Zero Rises

Lelouch dons the mask of Zero and builds the Black Knights, a rebel army fighting for the liberation of Japan from Britannia. His best friend Suzaku stands on the opposing side.

Act 3: Betrayal and Collapse

Secrets unravel as Lelouch's identity is exposed, alliances shatter, and the people he trusted most become enemies.

Finale: Zero Requiem

Lelouch engineers the most shocking final gambit in anime history — sacrificing himself to create a world without hatred, with Suzaku condemned to live behind the mask of Zero forever.

Fun facts

Behind the chess-board of Code Geass.

Lelouch is one of anime's most strategic protagonists.

His chess-master approach to battle made him a fan-favorite "anti-hero" archetype that influenced countless later series.

The ending sparked massive fan debate.

The "Zero Requiem" ending is one of the most discussed anime finales of all time — with fans still arguing about what truly happened.

CLAMP designed the characters.

The iconic elongated character designs were created by the legendary manga artist group CLAMP.
