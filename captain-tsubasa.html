Captain Tsubasa

Tsubasa Ozora has a single dream: to win the World Cup for Japan. Since childhood he has eaten, slept, and breathed football — and the manga that inspired an entire generation of professional players across Europe and South America.

Story overview

The ball always rolls toward the goal.

Arc 1: Elementary School & Nankatsu

Tsubasa moves to Nankatsu and meets rivals Genzo Wakabayashi — the goalkeeper with unprecedented talent — and Kojiro Hyuga, a powerhouse striker from poverty. Their first clashes define the series' emotional core.

Arc 2: Junior High Nationals

Tsubasa leads Nankatsu SC through Japan's junior high football tournament, cementing his status as the country's greatest young talent and earning international attention.

Arc 3: Youth World Cup

Japan competes in the World Youth Championship, coming up against elite European and Brazilian clubs. Tsubasa finally faces football on a global stage and must grow beyond his native abilities.

Beyond: Professional Europe

Later arcs follow Tsubasa and his rivals playing professionally in Brazil and Europe, building toward the ultimate goal of winning the FIFA World Cup for Japan.

Fun facts

The manga that built real-world footballers.

Real professionals credit Captain Tsubasa as their inspiration.

Players including Zinedine Zidane, Fernando Torres, Didier Drogba, and Hidetoshi Nakata have cited the manga as a reason they started playing football.

The series has been running since 1981.

Yoichi Takahashi's manga has spawned multiple anime adaptations, video games, and spin-offs over more than four decades.

Signature moves are deliberately superhuman.

The "Overhead Kick" and "Drive Shot" are physically impossible — but that's the point. The series made children believe football could be magical.
