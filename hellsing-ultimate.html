Hellsing Ultimate

The Hellsing Organisation exists to protect England from the undead and servants of darkness. Their ultimate weapon: Alucard — a vampire of incalculable age and power who serves the organisation queen, while secretly pursuing something far more personal than duty.

Suggested rating: TV-MA Suggested age: 18+ Content note: extreme violence, gore

Core Themes

  • What it means to be a true monster
  • Power, freedom, and the choice to serve
  • The thin line between humanity and the undead

Story overview

England's last defence — and its most terrifying weapon.

Act 1: The Hellsing Organisation

Police girl Seras Victoria is turned into a vampire by Alucard after a tragic encounter. She adjusts to her new existence while the Hellsing Organisation battles a rising tide of artificial "freak" vampires — something more dangerous than normal.

Act 2: Millennium

A Nazi organisation called Millennium — which survived WWII in secret — re-emerges with an army of vampire soldiers and a plan to start a war that will recreate the chaos of the Second World War. Their target: England.

Act 3: London Burns

Millennium launches its attack on London with an airship armada. The resulting battle is one of the most spectacular and brutal in anime — Hellsing, the Vatican's Iscariot division, and Millennium clash in total war.

Finale: Alucard's True Power

The final confrontation reveals the full depth of Alucard's nature, his history as Vlad the Impaler, and what he has been waiting for across centuries. The conclusion is operatic, strange, and unforgettable.

Fun facts

Behind the blood and bombast.

10 OVA episodes released over 7 years (2006–2012).

Hellsing Ultimate was released as an OVA series rather than a TV show, allowing for longer runtime and significantly higher production values per episode.

Alucard is Dracula spelled backwards.

The character is explicitly Vlad the Impaler / Dracula — this is not a secret within the series's fiction, and his true history plays a central role in the finale.

The Major's "I love war" speech is a cult classic.

The villain's extended monologue about his love of war — delivered before the London attack — is considered one of anime's greatest villain speeches and is regularly quoted online.
