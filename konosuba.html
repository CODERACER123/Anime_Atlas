KonoSuba

When shut-in Kazuma dies embarrassingly and a goddess offers him any one item to bring to the fantasy world, he picks her — Aqua, the goddess herself. Unfortunately, she's useless. His party fills out with a masochist crusader, an explosion-obsessed mage, and zero actual adventuring competence.

  • The isekai genre satirised from inside

Story overview

The Demon King won't be defeated by this party. Probably.

Act 1: The Worst Party in the World

Kazuma, Aqua, archpriestess Darkness, and explosion mage Megumin take on quests that should be manageable — and consistently fail, embarrass themselves, and somehow survive anyway through luck and spite.

Act 2: Aqua's Debts

Aqua, despite being a goddess, has no practical skills, spends all their money, and generates more problems than she solves. The show wrings endless comedy from her divine arrogance crashing against genuine uselessness.

Act 3: Generals of the Demon King

Various Demon King generals are defeated — almost by accident. Each villain is more peculiar than the last, and the "victories" are rarely heroic. KonoSuba consistently subverts the satisfaction loop of classic isekai.

Film: Crimson Legend

The series' theatrical film follows Megumin's backstory, her home village, and a villain tied to her past — while maintaining the series' comedic energy and expanding her character meaningfully.

Fun facts

The isekai that laughed at itself first.

KonoSuba is a cornerstone isekai parody.

Premiering in 2016 as the genre was reaching peak saturation, it immediately became the template for isekai self-awareness — every trope is present but deliberately subverted.

The low-budget animation style became part of the charm.

Season 1 was produced on a notoriously limited budget, resulting in intentionally off-model character art. Fans embraced it as part of the show's identity rather than a flaw.

A spin-off series follows Megumin before joining the party.

An Explosion on This Wonderful World! (2023) is a prequel following Megumin's days at wizard school — maintaining the parent series' comedy tone.
