The Rising of the Shield Hero

College student Naofumi Iwatani is summoned to a fantasy world as the legendary Shield Hero — only to be immediately framed and stripped of everything. Despised and alone, he must rise from nothing to become the world's true savior.

Story overview

Betrayed, rebuilt, and unstoppable.

Act 1: A Hero Betrayed

Naofumi is summoned alongside three other heroes but is immediately framed for a crime he didn't commit by the princess Malty. With no money, no party, and no trust, he buys a demi-human slave named Raphtalia to survive.

Act 2: The Shield's True Power

While other heroes focus on flashy attacks, Naofumi develops defensive power and strategic cunning. His bond with Raphtalia and young Filo grows into a genuine family unit forged through hardship.

Act 3: Waves of Catastrophe

Interdimensional waves of monsters periodically invade. Naofumi's group consistently outperforms the other heroes, who become reckless and careless with their privileged status.

Beyond: The Spiral of Providence

The true cause of the waves and the deeper conspiracy behind the four heroes' summoning begins to unfold — placing Naofumi at the centre of a much larger cosmic conflict.

Fun facts

Behind the shield and the stigma.

Naofumi is one of anime's most compelling "anti-heroes."

Starting as a genuinely cynical, bitter protagonist — rather than an optimistic one — gave the series a darker emotional tone unusual for isekai.

Raphtalia became hugely popular immediately.

She topped best-girl polls in Japan and internationally the season the anime aired, resonating with viewers for her loyalty and character development.

The anime adaptation boosted light novel sales massively.

After the 2019 anime, the source light novel and manga saw sales increases of over 400% — one of the biggest isekai boosts of that era.
