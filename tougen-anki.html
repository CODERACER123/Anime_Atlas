Tougen Anki

High schooler Shiki Ichinose discovers his family has secretly hunted demons for generations — and that he himself carries the blood of an ancient oni. Recruited into a hidden world of demon clans and clan hunters, he must choose which side of his heritage to honour.

Story overview

A new warrior entering a very old war.

Act 1: Awakening

Shiki's normal school life ends when a demon attacks and he awakens latent power — discovering his late grandfather was a legendary demon-slayer and that the organisation he worked with still exists, and still needs warriors.

Act 2: The Oni Clans

The demon world is not monolithic — it is divided into ancient clans with their own politics, aims, and codes. Shiki's hybrid heritage gives him unique access that neither pure humans nor pure demons possess.

Act 3: The Hidden War

A faction seeks to make the human-demon conflict open and absolute. Shiki's group must work across bloodline divisions to prevent a catastrophe that would shatter the hidden balance of both worlds.

Ongoing: Choosing a Side

As Shiki grows stronger, the question of his ultimate allegiance becomes urgent — not just politically but personally. The relationships he builds across clan lines define what he is willing to fight and die for.

Fun facts

Behind the oni action.

The manga serialises in Weekly Shonen Jump.

Tougen Anki is part of Jump's ongoing effort to diversify its supernatural action line, sitting alongside Jujutsu Kaisen and other titan titles in the same weekly magazine.

Japanese folklore provides its demon taxonomy.

The oni clans are rooted in genuine Japanese mythological tradition — the series does research-based worldbuilding rather than inventing its own creature rules from scratch.

The art is praised for its kinetic action sequences.

Creator Yura Urushibara's panel work translated particularly well to anime — the manga's dynamic fight choreography was a key selling point for adaptation.
