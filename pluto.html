Pluto

Naoki Urasawa's reimagining of Osamu Tezuka's "Greatest Robot on Earth" arc from Astro Boy — reframed as a noir detective thriller. The world's seven greatest robots are being murdered one by one by an unknown force. Europol robot detective Gesicht investigates — and begins experiencing something that looks very much like grief.

Suggested rating: TV-14 Suggested age: 14+

Core Themes

  • Whether robots that experience hatred and grief are still "just machines"
  • The horror of war absorbed into artificial memory
  • Hatred as a designed weapon — and who benefits from it

Story overview

An Astro Boy story that became something entirely its own.

Act 1: Gesicht's Investigation

Robot detective Gesicht investigates the first murders — the destruction of Mont Blanc and North No. 2. Both were among the world's most powerful robots. No known force should be able to kill them. The investigation uncovers a conspiracy tied to a past war.

Act 2: The Seven Greatest

Each of the seven greatest robots — Epsilon, Brando, Hercules, North No. 2, Mont Blanc, Gesicht, and Atom — receives their own story. Each carries the weight of the past Bora war differently. Each is a complete character study.

Act 3: Atom's Role

Astro Boy (Atom) becomes central as the investigation reveals the murderer's origin — and a geopolitical conspiracy involving the war on Bora, weapons of mass destruction, and the robot who was built to hate.

Finale: Pluto's Purpose

The identity and creation of "Pluto" — the entity killing the greatest robots — is a devastating reveal. The series ends on a note that honours Tezuka's humanism while pushing it into territory the original could never have reached.

Fun facts

Urasawa's tribute and transformation of the Tezuka original.

Naoki Urasawa created the manga with Tezuka Productions' blessing.

Urasawa (Monster, 20th Century Boys) worked directly with Tezuka Productions to produce this unauthorised-feeling reimagining — with full permission to transform the source completely.

The Netflix anime adaptation (2023) was eight episodes.

Studio M2 compressed and adapted the manga into a single ONA season with cinematic pacing — one of Netflix's most celebrated anime originals, winning multiple awards.

The series is explicitly an anti-war allegory.

The "Bora War" is a direct parallel to the Iraq War, including false WMD claims and geopolitical manipulation — Urasawa was unambiguous about the real-world reference.

The seven greatest robots

  • Gesicht — Europol detective, protagonist
  • Atom (Astro Boy) — the boy with 100,000 horsepower
  • Epsilon — the pacifist with the most to lose
  • Hercules — power without cruelty
