Berserk

Story overview

A sword, a brand, and an unbreakable will to survive.

Act 1: The Black Swordsman

Guts roams the world as a ruthless lone mercenary, slaying Apostles — demonic beings who sacrificed their humanity for power. He carries a Brand of Sacrifice that draws evil to him every night.

Act 2: The Golden Age

A flashback reveals Guts’ past: his rise through mercenary ranks, his bond with the charismatic Griffith, and his place in the Band of the Hawk — the greatest mercenary company of the era.

Act 3: The Eclipse

Griffith sacrifices the entire Band of the Hawk to become one of the God Hand — transcendent demonic angels. Only Guts and Casca survive, scarred beyond measure.

Act 4: The Falcon of the Millennium Empire

Guts builds a new found-family of companions and fights toward Griffith, now reborn as a godlike figure ruling a nation. The world between humanity and the supernatural begins to dissolve.

Fun facts

Behind the darkness of Kentaro Miura’s masterwork.

Created by Kentaro Miura, who passed in 2021.

Miura worked on Berserk for over 30 years. After his passing, his close friend and studio Gaga Studio continue the manga using his notes.

The Dragonslayer sword is iconic.

Guts’ enormous iron sword — described as “too big to be called a sword” — has become one of the most recognizable weapons in all of fiction.

Widely considered one of the greatest manga ever made.

Berserk has influenced countless dark fantasy works, from Dark Souls to Attack on Titan, and is cited as a benchmark for mature storytelling in manga.
