I Want to Eat Your Pancreas

An introverted, socially disengaged high school student accidentally discovers that his cheerful, popular classmate Sakura is secretly dying of a pancreatic disease — and that he's the only person outside her family who knows. A profound and unlikely friendship begins.

Suggested rating: PG-13 Suggested age: 13+

Core Themes

  • Living fully in the time you have
  • Finding connection across opposite personalities
  • The people we become because of those we love

Story overview

A secret, a friendship, and a limited amount of time.

Act 1: The Diary Is Found

The unnamed narrator finds Sakura's personal diary — titled "Living with Dying" — in a hospital waiting room. When she catches him reading it, rather than panicking, she decides to keep him close. He becomes both her confidant and her companion.

Act 2: Learning to Live

Sakura drags her reluctant companion into trips, restaurants, and experiences — determined to live richly despite her prognosis. He begins to change in ways neither of them expected, gradually learning what it means to genuinely want another person in his world.

Act 3: What Living Together Means

The friendship deepens beyond either character's defences. The tension between his emotional detachment and her warm, determined vitality becomes the emotional centre of the film.

Finale: The Ending Nobody Expected

The film upends audience expectations about how Sakura's story ends — and then, in the aftermath, forces the narrator and the audience to reckon with the meaning of everything that came before.

Fun facts

Behind the title that everyone questions and then understands.

The title comes from an ancient belief about eating organs.

In Japanese folk tradition, eating a sick person's corresponding organ would cure that illness — and it carries a deeper emotional meaning in the film's finale.

Based on a light novel that became a bestseller.

Yoru Sumino's debut novel sold over 3 million copies in Japan and was adapted into both this anime film (2018) and a live-action film (2017).

The narrator has no given name throughout.

He's referred to only by Sakura's pet names for him ("Shiga-kun," "Haruki") or simply as "the boy" — a deliberate authorial choice about identity and connection.

Key characters

  • Sakura Yamauchi — terminally ill, radiant, determined
  • The narrator (Haruki Shiga) — isolated, changed by Sakura
  • Kyoko — Sakura's devoted best friend
