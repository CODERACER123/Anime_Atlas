Rascal Does Not Dream of Bunny Girl Senpai

High schooler Sakuta Azusagawa encounters senior celebrity Mai Sakurajima dressed as a bunny girl — whom no one else can see. their search for answers pulls them into the strange science of "Adolescence Syndrome."

Core Themes

  • The invisible pain of adolescence
  • Quantum physics as emotional metaphor
  • Recognising and valuing others

Story overview

Science, feelings, and the girl no one could see.

Arc 1: Mai Sakurajima

Sakuta discovers that Mai, a famous actress on hiatus, is slowly disappearing from everyone's perception. Treating the phenomenon like a physics problem, he works to make her visible again — and falls for her in the process.

Arc 2: Tomoe's Loop

Classmate Tomoe is trapped in a time loop caused by her own unwillingness to move on from a painful social situation. Sakuta helps her face the truth, even at personal cost.

Arc 3: Rio's Doubles

Sakuta's science-club friend Rio splits into two versions of herself — each representing a different internal conflict she refuses to confront.

Arc 4 & Beyond: Kaede and Nodoka

Sakuta's younger sister Kaede is also afflicted by Adolescence Syndrome. And Mai's half-sister Nodoka begins body-swapping with Mai, unable to cope with her jealousy and longing.

Fun facts

Behind the science of adolescent hearts.

Inspired by real quantum physics concepts.

Each arc loosely references a physics phenomenon — Schrödinger's Cat, Laplace's Demon, the Doppler effect — used as emotional metaphors.

Sakuta is refreshingly direct.

Unlike most anime protagonists, Sakuta openly confesses his feelings and speaks bluntly — making him one of the most beloved romance leads in recent anime.

The film continues the story.

"Rascal Does Not Dream of a Dreaming Girl" is an essential film sequel that closes the story arcs left open in the series.
