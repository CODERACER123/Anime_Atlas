Bungo Stray Dogs

Atsushi Nakajima is kicked out of his orphanage and nearly starves to death before the Armed Detective Agency recruits him — a group of supernaturally gifted investigators. Their powers are named after real literary authors, as are those of their criminal rivals, the Port Mafia. Great literature never mentioned all this paperwork.

Story overview

Detectives, mobsters, and gifted humans who are both.

Act 1: Joining the Agency

Atsushi discovers his power (white tiger transformation) and is taken in by the oddly cheerful detective Osamu Dazai and his grumpy partner Kunikida. Early cases establish the city's dangerous balance between the Agency and the Port Mafia.

Act 2: Agency vs. Mafia

Port Mafia executive Akutagawa hunts Atsushi relentlessly. Dazai's own complicated past — he used to be a Port Mafia executive — becomes increasingly relevant as both organisations are drawn into a larger conflict.

Act 3: The Guild and the Rats

Foreign organisation The Guild arrives with its own agenda. Then the Decay of the Angel and the Rats in the House of the Dead emerge — criminal masterminds whose plan targets the Agency's very right to exist.

Ongoing: Fukuchi's Plan

Later seasons escalate to a global conspiracy involving the United Nations and a weapon that can rewrite the past. The series' blend of dark plotting and sharp comedy remains consistent throughout its run.

Fun facts

Literature, crime, and supernatural detective work.

Every character's ability is named after a real literary work.

Dazai's "No Longer Human" is named after his real-world counterpart's most famous novel. Atsushi's "Beast Beneath the Moonlight," Akutagawa's "Rashomon" — the series is a love letter to Japanese literature.

The series blends tonal extremes unusually well.

Bungo Stray Dogs can shift from genuinely menacing violence to absurdist physical comedy within the same episode — without either element undercutting the other.

A theatrical film, Dead Apple, bridges Seasons 2 and 3.

Released in 2018, the film focuses on Dazai and Shibusawa Tatsuhiko in a story that expands the lore of gifted human abilities and their limits.
