Spirited Away

Ten-year-old Chihiro stumbles into a magical spirit world and must work in a bathhouse run by a witch to rescue her parents who have been turned into pigs.

Suggested rating: G Suggested age: 8+

Core Themes

  • Courage in an unfamiliar world
  • Identity and remembering your name
  • Hard work and perseverance

Story overview

A young girl lost in a world of spirits and magic.

Act 1: Lost in the Spirit World

While moving to a new home, Chihiro's family stumbles into an abandoned theme park that transforms into a spirit world at night. Her parents eat enchanted food and are turned into pigs by the witch Yubaba.

Act 2: Working at the Bathhouse

To survive, Chihiro takes a job at Yubaba's magical bathhouse, losing her name and becoming "Sen." She befriends the mysterious Haku and the masked spirit No-Face.

Act 3: Trials and Transformation

Sen helps a giant baby, faces No-Face's hunger, and travels to the swamp where Yubaba's twin sister Zeniba lives. Each challenge reveals Sen's growing courage and compassion.

Finale: Remembering Who You Are

Chihiro recovers her true name, breaks the curse on her parents, and returns to the human world — forever changed by her journey through the spirit realm.

Fun facts

Behind the magic of Miyazaki's masterpiece.

First anime film to win an Academy Award.

Spirited Away won the Oscar for Best Animated Feature in 2003, a landmark moment for anime's global recognition.

The bathhouse is a real cultural reference.

Miyazaki based the spirit bathhouse on traditional Japanese public bathhouses (sentō), reflecting Shinto themes of purification.

No-Face represents modern consumerism.

The silent masked spirit who devours everything symbolizes loneliness and the emptiness of materialism.

  • Yubaba — the powerful witch who runs the bathhouse
