Your Name.

Tokyo boy Taki and rural girl Mitsuha randomly swap bodies periodically while sleeping, leaving each other notes and reshaping each other's lives — until the swaps abruptly stop, triggering a desperate search that reveals a heartbreaking truth.

Suggested rating: PG Suggested age: 10+

Core Themes

  • Fate and the invisible red thread of connection
  • Memory and the feeling of forgetting someone important
  • Rural vs. urban dreams and identity

Story overview

Two strangers, one connection, across time.

Act 1: Waking Up in Someone Else's Life

Taki wakes as Mitsuha in her small mountain town; Mitsuha wakes as Taki in Tokyo. They begin leaving instructions for each other via phone notes, causing comedic and touching disruptions in each other's lives.

Act 2: The Swaps Stop

Suddenly, the swaps cease. Taki reaches out to Mitsuha but gets no response — and he realises he can't even find her town on a map. Something is deeply wrong.

Act 3: The Comet's Secret

Taki discovers the devastating truth: Mitsuha's town was destroyed three years ago by a comet fragment. He and Mitsuha have been swapping across time — not just distance.

Finale: Twilight, Kuchikamizake, and a Name

In the fleeting "kataware-doki" twilight, they momentarily meet in each other's bodies at the mountain's peak. Their race to warn the townspeople and remember each other forms one of cinema's most emotionally powerful conclusions.

Fun facts

Behind the comet and the red thread.

Highest-grossing anime film of all time at release (2016).

Your Name. surpassed Spirited Away's worldwide gross when it released, becoming a global phenomenon and introducing millions to anime film.

The RADWIMPS soundtrack is inseparable from the film.

The Japanese rock band RADWIMPS composed an original score that fans still cite as one of the greatest in anime history.

Makoto Shinkai wrote and directed solo.

The combination of his visual artistry and storytelling made him the first anime director after Miyazaki to achieve this level of global mainstream recognition.

Key story elements

  • Musubi — the Shinto concept of connection/fate
  • Kuchikamizake — sake made from chewing rice
  • Kataware-doki — twilight, between worlds
  • The red braided cord (kumihimo)
