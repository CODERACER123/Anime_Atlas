Blue Exorcist

Rin Okumura has always been troublesome, hot-tempered, and oddly strong. When he manifests blue flames and learns he is the son of Satan, raised by the kind exorcist Father Fujimoto, he makes a choice: he will become an exorcist and defeat his own father. Training at True Cross Academy — where his twin Yukio already teaches — is the first step.

Core Themes

  • Identity beyond bloodline — you are not what you were born
  • Two brothers carrying the same heritage in opposite directions
  • Power that is also a mark — something everyone can see about you

Story overview

The son of Satan learning to be the thing that fights him.

Act 1: Awakening and Loss

Rin's blue flames manifest in a demonic attack, and, crucially, Father Fujimoto — his guardian, mentor, and the only father he's known — dies trying to protect him. The grief and guilt of that loss drives Rin's determination throughout the series.

Act 2: True Cross Academy

Rin enrols in the exorcist training division of True Cross Academy, where his brother Yukio is already a qualified teacher. Their dynamic — Rin's chaotic power vs. Yukio's controlled perfectionism — is the series' central tension.

Act 3: The Order's Suspicion

The True Cross Order knows what Rin is. Some want to use him; some want to kill him. His classmates discover his heritage — and their varied responses define who he can trust. The politics of the exorcist world are not clean.

Ongoing: Yukio's Darkness

Later arcs focus increasingly on Yukio — who discovers he also carries Satan's blood, differently — and the series' most complex question: what happens when the person committed to fighting evil begins to understand its appeal?

Fun facts

Behind the blue flames and the two adaptations.

The original anime diverged from the manga in Season 1's finale.

The 2011 A-1 Pictures anime caught up to the manga and created an original ending. The 2017 series Kyoto Saga and subsequent seasons return to the manga's storyline, effectively ignoring the 2011 finale.

Yukio's arc became more prominent than expected.

In the manga, Yukio's internal conflict — struggling with the same satanic power he's spent his life denying — became the series' most discussed storyline, rivalling Rin's as the emotional core.

Rin's weapon, Kurikara, seals his demon form.

The katana sealing Rin's power belongs to Father Fujimoto — making it both the safeguard against his worst self and a direct link to the mentor he lost and is trying to honour.
