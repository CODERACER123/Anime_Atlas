Fruits Basket

Orphaned Tohru Honda stumbles into the Sohma household and discovers their family curse: 13 members are possessed by spirits of the Chinese zodiac and transform into their animal when hugged by the opposite sex. She resolves to break the curse — and heal them all.

Core Themes

  • Accepting people — and being accepted — without conditions
  • Abuse, trauma, and the long road to healing
  • Breaking cycles of pain passed down through families

Story overview

One girl's unconditional warmth against a century of pain.

Act 1: Meeting the Sohmas

Tohru moves into the Sohma house with Kyo (cat) and Yuki (rat). Her relentless optimism and genuine kindness are initially confusing to people raised in a household defined by conditional love and secrecy.

Act 2: The Zodiac Members

Each zodiac member is introduced — each carrying different emotional wounds inflicted by the curse's controlling "god," Akito Sohma. Tohru befriends each, slowly understanding what the curse has taken from them.

Act 3: Akito's True Nature

The series builds toward the revelation of Akito's identity, the nature of the bond binding the zodiac members, and whether anyone can choose to break free — or whether they're destined to stay cursed forever.

Finale: Breaking the Curse

Tohru's determination — and love for Kyo specifically — becomes the catalyst for the curse's undoing. The finale is earned, emotionally complex, and deeply satisfying across its three seasons.

Fun facts

Behind the curse and the healing.

The 2019 remake is a faithful, complete adaptation.

The original 2001 anime was beloved but couldn't complete the story. TMS Entertainment's 2019–2021 remake covered the full manga, including the dark third act the original missed.

Kyo vs. Yuki is among shojo's most contested rivalries.

The Kyo–Yuki–Tohru love triangle generated enormous fandom debate — team Kyo and team Yuki were genuine opposing camps in 2000s anime fandom.

The series has serious discussions of childhood abuse.

Each zodiac member's backstory involves control and conditional love — the series handles this with unusual care for a shojo title, not softening it.

The main zodiac trio

  • Tohru Honda — the healer, unconditional kindness
  • Kyo Sohma — the cat, rage as defence
  • Yuki Sohma — the rat, cold grace hiding loneliness
  • Akito Sohma — the god, their tormentor
