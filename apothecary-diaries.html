The Apothecary Diaries

Maomao, a sharp-minded apothecary's daughter kidnapped into the imperial court, keeps her head down — until her medical expertise makes her indispensable as a food taster and mystery solver. Now she investigates the court's secrets whether she wants to or not.

Story overview

A reluctant sleuth in an imperial court full of secrets.

Arc 1: The Rear Palace Mysteries

Assigned as a food taster, Maomao notices that the Emperor's children are mysteriously falling ill. She anonymously leaves a note with the solution. When the flamboyant eunuch Jinshi tracks her down, her quiet life is over.

Arc 2: Jinshi's Investigations

Jinshi makes Maomao his personal attendant and repeatedly draws her into investigating court intrigues — poisonings, inheritance schemes, political marriages, and threats to the Emperor. Their relationship is complex and evolving.

Arc 3: The Outer Palace

Maomao is eventually assigned to the outer palace, working alongside soldiers and officials in a different power structure. New mysteries arise, and her knowledge of medicine proves useful in ways far beyond food tasting.

Unfolding: The Apothecary's Daughter

Secrets about Maomao's own origins — her father's past, her connection to the imperial family — slowly surface, making her central to the court's history in ways she never anticipated.

Fun facts

Behind the court and the cures.

Maomao's blush-suppression technique is a fan favourite.

Maomao deliberately self-induces skin reactions to make herself look less attractive and avoid unwanted attention — an oddly practical detail that resonated with viewers.

The fictional setting is heavily inspired by Tang Dynasty China.

The court hierarchy, customs, architecture, and medicine all draw on historical Chinese sources, giving the series an unusually grounded feel for a fantasy mystery.

One of the best-reviewed anime of 2023–24.

The Apothecary Diaries was praised uniformly for its writing, its female lead, and its refreshing genre blend — topping multiple seasonal rankings.
