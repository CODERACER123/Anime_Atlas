Tokyo Ghoul

Ordinary college student Ken Kaneki survives a ghoul attack and becomes a half-ghoul — forced to navigate a hidden underworld of predators and the humans who hunt them.

Story overview

Caught between humanity and monstrosity.

Act 1: The Attack and Transformation

Ken Kaneki goes on a date with the beautiful Rize — who turns out to be a ghoul. After a near-fatal attack, an emergency transplant leaves Kaneki with ghoul organs, making him a one-of-a-kind hybrid.

Act 2: Anteiku and Belonging

Kaneki is taken in by ghouls working at the café Anteiku, who try to coexist peacefully with humans. He struggles to accept his new nature while hiding it from his human friends.

Act 3: Capture and Breaking Point

Kaneki is captured and tortured by the ghoul Yamori (Jason) in a brutal arc that transforms his white hair and his psyche. He emerges colder, stronger, and fractured.

Finale: The Anteiku Raid

The CCG (ghoul-hunting agency) raids Anteiku. Kaneki throws himself into the chaos to protect the people he loves, leading to a devastating conclusion that resets everything he was.

Fun facts

Behind the mask of Tokyo Ghoul.

Kaneki's white hair transformation became iconic.

After his torture arc, Kaneki's hair turns white from stress — a visual symbol that became one of anime's most recognizable character designs.

The manga goes much deeper than the anime.

Fans widely recommend reading Sui Ishida's manga, which contains extensive lore and character development that the anime condensed or omitted.

The centipede imagery is symbolic.

Centipedes appear throughout as a symbol of Kaneki's trauma and the ghoul Rize who haunts his subconscious.
