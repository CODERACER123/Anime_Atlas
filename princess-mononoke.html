Princess Mononoke

Cursed by a dying demon god, young prince Ashitaka travels west to find a cure — and arrives amid a war between the industrialising Iron Town and the ancient gods of the forest, led by the wild girl San, raised by wolves.

Suggested rating: PG-13 Suggested age: 13+

Core Themes

  • Humanity's conflict with the natural world
  • No villains — only competing needs
  • Coexistence over conquest

Story overview

A war with no heroes and no monsters — only choices.

Act 1: The Curse Begins

Ashitaka kills a demon boar to save his village, but is cursed in the process. He leaves his home forever and heads west, seeking the Deer God — the only being who might lift the curse.

Act 2: Iron Town & Lady Eboshi

Ashitaka finds Iron Town — a progressive, forward-thinking settlement providing sanctuary to outcasts and lepers, led by the brilliant Lady Eboshi. Her iron works, however, are destroying the forest gods.

Act 3: San, the Wolf-Raised Girl

Ashitaka is drawn to San — a human girl raised by wolf gods who sees all of humanity as the enemy. His ability to see without hate makes him the only bridge between both worlds.

Finale: The Great Forest Spirit

A human attempt to kill the Great Forest Spirit triggers a catastrophe that threatens all life. Ashitaka and San must work together to restore what's been lost — though they cannot undo what humanity has done.

Fun facts

Behind Miyazaki's masterpiece.

It was the highest-grossing Japanese film at the time of its 1997 release.

Princess Mononoke held that record until Titanic arrived in Japan shortly after — and then until Spirited Away surpassed it in 2001.

Miyazaki described it as his most personal and most difficult film.

He spent years developing the script and considers it the film where he most directly wrestled with his feelings about humanity and the natural world.

No purely evil characters exist.

Miyazaki insisted that Lady Eboshi, who destroys the forest, is not a villain — she's building a better society. The conflict is systemic, not moral.

The forest gods

  • Moro — wolf goddess, San's adoptive mother
  • Okkoto — ancient blind boar king
  • The Forest Spirit / Deer God
  • The Kodama — small but vital spirits
