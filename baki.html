Baki

Baki Hanma has trained his entire life in underground martial arts with a single goal: to defeat his father Yujiro Hanma — the strongest creature on Earth. His path is paved with brutal fights against the most powerful warriors in the world.

Suggested rating: TV-MA Suggested age: 17+

Core Themes

  • Pushing the human body to its absolute peak
  • Father-son rivalry and toxic legacy
  • The philosophy of strength

Story overview

The strongest son vs. the strongest man alive.

Arc 1: Death Row Convicts

Five of the world's most dangerous death-row convicts escape and arrive in Tokyo seeking to taste defeat — having never felt it. Baki and his allies must stop them in brutal one-on-one fights.

Arc 2: The Great Chinese Challenge

Masters of Chinese martial arts challenge Japan's top fighters. Ancient techniques clash with modern strength in a series of intense matches that test fundamentals against innovation.

Arc 3: Retsu Goes to America

Chinese martial artist Retsu enters professional boxing to prove the superiority of ancient fighting arts over modern combat sports — with wild results.

Arc 4: Father vs. Son

The series builds toward the inevitable showdown between Baki and Yujiro — a fight decades in the making that asks whether strength can ever be separated from love and hatred.

Fun facts

Behind the impossible muscles of Baki.

The series has been running since 1991.

Mangaka Keisuke Itagaki has been publishing Baki in various forms for over 30 years, with multiple sequel series continuing the story.

Anatomy is deliberately exaggerated for effect.

Characters develop muscle groups that don't exist in reality — this is intentional, reinforcing the superhuman nature of the series' world.

Real martial arts are researched and depicted.

From judo to boxing to capoeira to ancient Chinese styles, Baki incorporates real technique into its fights, making them educational as well as absurd.
