Boruto: Naruto Next Generations

Boruto Uzumaki grows up in a world his father Naruto helped build — but rather than feeling pride, he resents being in the Hokage's shadow. His path as a ninja will carve out an identity entirely his own.

Story overview

The next generation inherits a changed world.

Act 1: Academy & Graduation

Boruto attends ninja school in a Hidden Leaf Village that now has trains and electronic devices. He chafes against tradition and cheats during the chunin exams — only to face consequences that redefine his values.

Act 2: Kara Organization

A new villain group, Kara, emerges with scientific ninja tools and an agenda involving the ancient being Isshiki Otsutsuki. The threats are unlike anything the previous generation faced.

Act 3: Karma and the Otsutsuki

Boruto and Kawaki both carry Karma — a genetic mark left by Otsutsuki beings — that slowly transforms their bodies and brings cosmic-scale consequences to the village.

Boruto: Two Blue Vortex

A time-skip continuation pushes Boruto into a darker, more mature story as he operates as a fugitive while Kawaki has become the village's protector — and the two are on a collision course.

Fun facts

Behind the next generation of ninja.

Written initially by Naruto creator Masashi Kishimoto.

Kishimoto took over writing duties from Ukyo Kodachi partway through the manga, steering it toward the darker Two Blue Vortex arc.

The anime added substantial filler arcs.

The original anime series is known for a high volume of filler content; many fans recommend watching only canon episodes for a better experience.

The opening flash-forward set high expectations.

Episode 1 opens with a flash-forward to a destroyed village and an older, scarred Boruto fighting Kawaki — immediately signalling a much darker future story.
