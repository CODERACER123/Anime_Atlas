Darwin's Game

Kaname Sudou accepts an invite to a mobile game called Darwin's Game — and immediately an opponent tries to kill him. He's trapped: after your first battle you cannot leave. Every player has a unique power called a Sigil, and the game's currency is human lives.

Suggested rating: TV-MA Suggested age: 17+ Content note: graphic violence

Story overview

One message. One game. No exit.

Act 1: First Blood

Kaname's first encounter leaves him shaken — and kills a friend. His Sigil (the ability to curse any weapon he touches) triggers under extreme stress. He realises he has to fight or die, and chooses to fight smart rather than rely on brute strength.

Act 2: Clan Building

Kaname forms the Sunset Ravens clan — recruiting Shuka (chains), Ryuuji (poison), and others. Darwin's Game rewards cooperation as much as individual strength, and Kaname's strategic thinking becomes the clan's greatest asset.

Act 3: Shibuya Raid Event

A large-scale survival event traps all players in Shibuya for a limited time, with powerful items as prizes. The Sunset Ravens, rival clans, and the game's mysterious organisers all collide in one contained catastrophe.

Ongoing: The Game Master

The true purpose of Darwin's Game — why it exists, who runs it, and what "winning" even means — begins to emerge. Kaname's goal shifts from surviving to dismantling the entire operation.

Fun facts

Behind the mobile app of death.

The anime adapts the manga by FLIPFLOPs.

Darwin's Game as a manga has been serialising since 2012 in Weekly Shonen Champion — the anime (Nexus, 2020) compressed a long arc into a sharp 11-episode run.

Every Sigil is unique to its user.

The game grants each player a bespoke supernatural power — ranging from combat abilities to information powers to environmental control — creating wildly varied matchups.

Shuka became an instant fan favourite.

Chain-wielding Shuka's debut fight — against multiple opponents simultaneously before the opening credits — became one of 2020's most shared anime clips on social media.
