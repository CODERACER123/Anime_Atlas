Hajime no Ippo

Bullied high schooler Ippo Makunouchi is saved from a beating by pro boxer Mamoru Takamura — and the punch Ippo throws back at a punching bag changes both their lives. A long, emotional journey to the top of Japanese boxing begins.

Story overview

The gentlest boy. The hardest puncher.

Act 1: Finding His Reason to Fight

Ippo joins Kamogawa Boxing Gym and trains under the legendary old coach Genji Kamogawa. His debut fight against prodigy Miyata shows both his raw grit and the long road ahead. He wins — barely.

Act 2: Climbing the Japanese Ranks

Opponent after opponent tests Ippo in different ways — power, speed, technique, psychology. Each fight is a complete story. The series never lets Ippo feel invincible; every win costs something.

Act 3: Japanese Featherweight Champion

Ippo eventually captures the Japanese featherweight title in one of the series' most cathartic fights. But champions defend — and defending is harder than becoming one.

Ongoing: World Stage

With over 1,400 manga chapters, the series continues to follow Ippo through losses, comebacks, and the question of whether a fundamentally kind person can sustain the ruthlessness required to be world champion.

Fun facts

Behind the longest-running sports manga.

The manga has been serialising since 1989 — still ongoing.

George Morikawa's Hajime no Ippo is one of the longest-running manga in history, with over 1,400 chapters accumulated across more than 35 years.

Boxing technique is extensively researched.

The Dempsey Roll, in-fighting pressure, and footwork are depicted with remarkable technical accuracy — the series is regularly cited by actual boxing fans as the most credible sports manga.

The humour is as legendary as the fights.

The gym's comedic culture — especially Takamura's outrageous personality — provides constant relief between brutal bouts. Fans who only know it as a "boxing manga" often miss how genuinely funny it is.
