Cyberpunk: Edgerunners

In the ruthless Night City, street kid David Martinez installs a military-grade cyberware and becomes a mercenary, chasing glory in a world designed to chew people up and spit them out.

Story overview

Neon lights, chrome bodies, and shattered dreams.

Act 1: Night City Eats You Alive

David Martinez is a scholarship kid in a megacorp-run school, living with his mother in the slums. After a tragedy, he impulsively installs a military Sandevistan cyberware and gets noticed by mercenaries.

Act 2: Becoming an Edgerunner

David joins Maine's mercenary crew and falls for the tech-savvy info-broker Lucy, who dreams of escaping to the Moon. He earns his place through recklessness and raw talent.

Act 3: Too Much Chrome

As David piles on more cyberware, the line between human and machine blurs. The crew begins to fracture, and corpo forces hunt them down.

Finale: A Promise to the Moon

David pushes himself past his limits to protect Lucy in a devastating climax that perfectly captures Night City's brutal promise: glory comes at the cost of everything.

Fun facts

Behind the neon of Studio Trigger's masterwork.

It revived the Cyberpunk 2077 player base overnight.

After Edgerunners released on Netflix, Cyberpunk 2077 saw its highest concurrent player count since launch — a rare example of an anime saving a video game.

Produced by Studio Trigger.

The studio behind Kill la Kill and Promare brought their signature kinetic animation style to the Cyberpunk universe.

The "I really want to stay at your house" scene became iconic.

The ending paired with that song became one of 2022's most emotionally devastating anime moments online.
