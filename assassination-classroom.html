Assassination Classroom

A tentacled creature destroys part of the moon and announces he will do the same to Earth — but first, he'll teach Class 3-E at Kunugigaoka Junior High. His students have one year to kill him, or Earth is destroyed. The catch: he's the best teacher any of them have ever had.

Story overview

The teacher who must be killed — and why you'll cry anyway.

Act 1: Meet Korosensei

Class 3-E — the school's "end class," treated as failures — receives a yellow, tentacled teacher who can move at Mach 20. He's warm, insightful, and genuinely invested in each student's growth. Their assassination attempts grow increasingly creative.

Act 2: The Class Finds Their Feet

Under Korosensei's teaching, the students of Class E begin to excel — academically, personally, and in their assassination techniques. They start to resent the school system that labeled them failures more than they resent their target.

Act 3: Rivals and Government Agents

Professional assassins, rival teachers with dark methods, and a government desperate for results all enter the picture. The class must defend their teacher as much as they plot to kill him.

Finale: The Truth Behind Korosensei

The origin of Korosensei — who he was before the tentacles — is revealed in one of anime's most emotionally devastating backstory arcs. The final act is heartbreaking and earned.

Fun facts

Behind the tentacles and tears.

Korosensei is consistently voted one of anime's best teachers — and characters.

Despite being the target of assassination, his genuine care for students made him one of the most beloved characters in mid-2010s anime.

The series is a sharp satire of Japan's education system.

The stratification of students into "elite" and "end" classes mirrors real issues in competitive Japanese schooling — the series critiques this without preaching.

Karma Akabane became a fan-favourite immediately.

The class's most violent and intelligent student struck readers and viewers as a morally complex breakout character from his very first appearance.
