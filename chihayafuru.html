Chihayafuru

Chihaya Ayase is obsessed with karuta — a card game based on classical Japanese poetry where two players face 50 cards each and must grab the correct card the instant the first syllable of a poem is read. It is blindingly fast, deeply strategic, and profoundly connected to Japanese literary tradition. Chihaya intends to become the best in Japan.

Core Themes

  • Karuta as a bridge between modern Japan and its poetic past
  • Three people whose paths keep crossing but can never fully align

Story overview

Three seasons of cards, poems, and quietly devastating feelings.

Act 1: The Childhood Promise

In primary school, Chihaya befriends transfer student Arata — a karuta prodigy whose grandfather is a living legend of the game. He introduces her and class outcast Taichi to competitive karuta. They promise to meet again at the top. Then Arata moves away.

Act 2: Mizusawa Karuta Club

In high school, Chihaya starts a karuta club with Taichi and recruits three other members. The team's growth — each character developing their own style and philosophy of the game — forms the sports anime backbone of the series.

Act 3: The Queen and the Master

The Queen (top female player) and Master (top male player) matches are the summit of competitive karuta. As Chihaya and Arata both approach those heights, the romantic triangle between Chihaya, Taichi, and Arata becomes increasingly painful.

Ongoing: What Karuta Means

Each character asks a different version of the same question: why do I play? The series' answer — that some things are worth pursuing even without a guaranteed payoff — gives it emotional stakes beyond any single match.

Fun facts

Behind the card-slapping and the century-old poetry.

Karuta is based on real Hyakunin Isshu poetry.

The 100 poems used in competitive karuta are the Ogura Hyakunin Isshu — an actual anthology compiled in the 13th century. The series subtly teaches viewers classical Japanese poetry.

The art style is unusually beautiful for a sports anime.

Madhouse's production treated karuta matches with the same visual intensity usually reserved for shonen fighting anime — slow-motion card strikes, atmospheric lighting, and careful attention to player expressions.

Three seasons were produced, running from 2011 to 2020.

The manga by Yuki Suetsugu has been serialising since 2008 — the anime condensed years of story with remarkable fidelity to the source's emotional rhythms.

The triangle

  • Chihaya Ayase — the player, karuta's living embodiment
  • Taichi Mashima — the tactician, carrying feelings he can't express
  • Arata Wataya — the prodigy, carrying his grandfather's legacy
