Erased

Struggling manga artist Satoru Fujinuma has an involuntary ability he calls "Revival" — being sent minutes back in time to prevent deaths. When his mother is murdered, Revival sends him back 18 years to 1988, where three of his classmates are about to be kidnapped and killed.

Story overview

Go back far enough to save the ones you failed.

Act 1: Revival in the Present

Adult Satoru lives a quiet, frustrated life until Revival forces him to save a child from being hit by a truck. When his mother is stabbed at home and he's framed for it, Revival kicks in — but sends him back eighteen years instead of minutes.

Act 2: 1988 — Protecting Kayo

Now in his 11-year-old body with his adult mind, Satoru focuses on Kayo Hinazuki — the first child victim and the girl he barely knew. By befriending and protecting her, he hopes to break the entire chain of murders.

Act 3: The Killer's Identity

Satoru begins to close in on the killer — someone embedded in the community, trusted by children. The show builds genuine dread as the killer seems always one step ahead.

Finale: Memory, Waking, and Justice

The final confrontation requires Satoru to lose everything — his time, his memory, his years — before the truth can be put into the light. The ending is quiet, earned, and bittersweet.

Fun facts

Behind the time-travel mystery.

One of the most watched anime of Winter 2016.

Erased aired to extraordinary viewership and discussion week by week, with each episode's cliffhangers driving massive online speculation.

The anime's ending differs from the manga.

The manga was still ongoing when the anime was produced, leading to a different, more compressed ending — one that's debated by fans to this day.

It was adapted into a live-action Netflix series.

Netflix produced a 12-episode live-action version in 2017 that follows the manga ending more closely, offering fans a different conclusion to compare.
