Cowboy Bebop

A ragtag crew of bounty hunters — Spike, Jet, Faye, Ed, and Ein — drift across a colonized solar system, chasing criminals and running from their own pasts.

Story overview

Three, two, one — let's jam.

The Bebop Crew

Former syndicate hitman Spike Spiegel and retired cop Jet Black operate the bounty hunter ship Bebop. They're soon joined by con artist Faye Valentine and genius hacker Ed, along with data dog Ein.

Episodic Adventures

Each episode blends genres — westerns, noir, horror, comedy — while chasing bounties across Mars, Venus, the asteroid belt, and beyond. The crew rarely catches their mark.

Spike's Past Resurfaces

Throughout the series, fragments of Spike's time in the Red Dragon Syndicate emerge, along with the woman he loved — Julia — and his nemesis Vicious, who now runs the syndicate.

Finale: You're Gonna Carry That Weight

Spike confronts Vicious in a final showdown that brings his old life crashing into the present. The ending remains one of anime's most poetic and debated conclusions.

Fun facts

Behind the jazz and stars of Cowboy Bebop.

The soundtrack is unlike any other anime.

Yoko Kanno and The Seatbelts composed a fusion of jazz, blues, rock, and classical music that defined the show's soul.

Often called the best anime ever made.

Cowboy Bebop regularly tops "greatest anime of all time" lists and is cited as many Western viewers' entry point into anime.

Every episode title is a music reference.

Episode names like "Sympathy for the Devil" and "Bohemian Rhapsody" reflect the show's deep musical DNA.
