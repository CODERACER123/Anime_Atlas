86 — Eighty Six

The Republic of San Magnolia claims its war is fought entirely by unmanned weapons with no casualties. The truth: the pilots are children of the "86th District" — a persecuted ethnic minority stripped of citizenship, piloting mechs to their deaths beyond the wall, hidden from the public eye.

Suggested rating: TV-MA Suggested age: 17+ Content note: war, systemic racism, death

Core Themes

  • Systemic racism made invisible through comfortable lies
  • Dignity in the face of a society that denies your humanity
  • The burden of surviving when others didn't

Story overview

The war the Republic pretends isn't happening — with children.

Act 1: Handler and Spearhead

Republic officer Lena Milizé becomes handler to the Spearhead Squadron — an 86 unit assigned the most dangerous missions as a death sentence. She is horrified by their conditions. They are used to people like her being horrified and doing nothing.

Act 2: Shin and the Reaper

Squadron captain Shin Nouzen — "the Reaper" — leads his unit with cold efficiency born from deep personal trauma. His relationship with Lena, conducted only through radio, gradually reveals both characters' full humanity.

Part 2: Beyond the Republic

The surviving 86 escape the Republic's control and are enlisted by the Giad Empire — a former enemy — to fight the Legion. Here, they must relearn what it means to be valued and to have a future.

Ongoing: The Legion's Origin

The autonomous Legion — machines that have been adding human brains to their network — has a creator, a purpose, and a final objective far more horrifying than anyone in either nation wants to admit.

Fun facts

Behind the war no one talks about.

One of the most acclaimed anime of 2021.

86 received near-universal praise for its thematic depth, A-1 Pictures' production values, and its willingness to confront discrimination as its central subject rather than a backdrop.

The original light novel was written by Asato Asato.

A rare case of a major light novel authored by a woman, dealing with serious political themes — it ran from 2017 and has collected multiple major light novel awards.

The split-cour broadcast created enormous anticipation.

A long, unexplained delay between Season 1's first and second halves became one of 2021's most discussed phenomena — partly due to production challenges that A-1 Pictures acknowledged.
