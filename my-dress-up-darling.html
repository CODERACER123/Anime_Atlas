My Dress-Up Darling

Shy doll-maker Wakana Gojo is asked to make a costume by his bold and popular classmate Marin Kitagawa — sparking a partnership built on craftsmanship, passion, and growing feelings.

Suggested rating: TV-14 Suggested age: 14+

Core Themes

  • Pursuing your passion without shame
  • Respect and support between partners
  • Self-confidence and creativity

Story overview

Needles, thread, and a blossoming romance.

Act 1: An Unlikely Request

Wakana Gojo has devoted himself to the traditional art of hina dolls, hiding his hobby from peers out of fear of being judged. When Marin discovers his skill, she asks him to make a cosplay costume — and his world opens up.

Act 2: First Costume, First Connection

The pair work late nights and weekends crafting Marin's first costume. Their shared dedication reveals complementary passions and builds a genuine friendship that quickly deepens.

Act 3: More Characters, More Costumes

New cosplayers join their circle. Each costume project comes with new challenges — obscure characters, limited materials, tight deadlines — and each one brings Gojo and Marin closer.

Ongoing: Feelings Take Shape

As Gojo's confidence grows and Marin's admiration deepens, the anime crafts one of the most beloved slow-burn romances in recent memory.

Fun facts

Behind the stitches of My Dress-Up Darling.

Uses real sewing and costume-making techniques.

The anime depicts actual dressmaking processes — pattern cutting, fabric selection, lining — giving the work genuine authenticity.

Marin became a cultural icon almost instantly.

After the anime aired, Marin Kitagawa topped character popularity polls worldwide and sparked a real-world surge in cosplay interest.

Gojo's doll-crafting is deeply researched.

The hina doll tradition depicted is a real and ancient Japanese craft; the anime portrays it with unusual depth and respect.
