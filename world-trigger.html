World Trigger

Four years ago a dimensional gate opened above Mikado City and Neighbors — beings from other worlds — began invading. The Border defence organisation protects Japan using Trigger weapons powered by Trion, a biological energy. New recruit Osamu Mikumo is weaker than almost every agent — but his alliance with Neighbor boy Yuma changes everything about how both sides understand each other.

Core Themes

  • Tactics over raw power — intelligence as the strongest Trigger
  • "Neighbors" as people, not monsters — the other side has its own logic
  • Institutional structures: Border's ranks, politics, and how good people navigate them

Story overview

A careful, tactical sci-fi series that rewards patience.

Act 1: Yuma and Osamu

Osamu's defining trait — choosing to act even knowing he'll lose — attracts Yuma Kuga, a Neighbor with a Black Trigger (a uniquely powerful, technically illegal weapon) who becomes his partner. Their unit is formed: Tamakoma Second.

Act 2: The Large-Scale Invasion

A massive coordinated Neighbor attack on Mikado City reveals that invaders have strategic intelligence and specific objectives — they're not random. Border barely holds. Osamu's inability to protect people he cares about in this arc becomes a core character wound.

Act 3: Rank Wars

Border's internal ranking system — four-team Rank Wars matches — forms the series' sports-anime backbone. Tamakoma Second is at the bottom and needs to climb. Each match is a tactical puzzle; preparation and analysis are as important as the combat itself.

Ongoing: Away Mission

The goal becomes joining a Border expedition into Neighbor space to find the force responsible for the attacks — and potentially the Neighbors who have abducted Mikado City residents. The political and personal stakes converge.

Fun facts

Behind the Trigger system and the long game.

World Trigger is exceptionally detailed in its tactical writing.

Daisuke Ashihara's manga is unusual for action shonen in that it shows characters preparing for fights, analysing opponents' Trigger loadouts, and adjusting strategies — the battles are won before they start.

The manga went on hiatus multiple times due to the author's health.

Ashihara has been transparent about health challenges affecting the serialisation — the manga's fans are notably patient and supportive, aware of the reason behind gaps.

Three seasons of anime exist, each improving in quality.

The original 73-episode run (2014–2016) had pacing and production issues. Seasons 2 and 3 (2021–2022, 12 episodes each) were widely considered significant improvements in adaptation quality.
